# BindFlow: A Free, User-Friendly Pipeline for Absolute Binding Free Energy Calculations Using Free Energy Perturbation or MM(PB/GB)SA

**BindFlow：一款免费、易用的计算绝对结合自由能的流程工具，支持自由能微扰法或MM(PB/GB)SA方法。**

## 精华总结
本研究建立了BindFlow作为绝对结合自由能计算的经过验证且易于使用的工具，在某些系统和力场上实现的相关性接近FEP方法，同时仅需要其一小部分的计算成本。

**关键词**: 结合自由能、分子动力学模拟、自由能微扰、MM(PB/GB)SA

## 摘要（英文）
We present BindFlow, a Python-based software for automated absolute binding free energy (ABFE) calculations at the free energy perturbation (FEP) or at the molecular mechanics Poisson-Boltzmann/generalized Born surface area [MM(PB/GB)SA] level of theory. BindFlow is free, open-source, user-friendly, easily customizable, runs on work-stations or distributed computing platforms, and provides extensive documentation and tutorials. BindFlow uses GROMACS as molecular dynamics engine and provides built-in support for the small-molecule force fields GAFF, OpenFF, and Espaloma. We test BindFlow by computing affinities for 139 ligand/target pairs, involving eight different targets including six soluble proteins, one membrane protein and one non-protein host– guest system. Quantified by Pearson, Kendall, and Spearman correlations coefficients, we find that the agreement of BindFlow predictions with experiments are overall similar to gold standards in the field. Interestingly, we find that MM(PB/GB)SA achieves correlations that, for some systems and force fields, approach those obtained with FEP, while requiring only a fraction of the computational cost. This study establishes BindFlow as a validated and accessible tool for ABFE calculations.

## 摘要（中文）
我们提出了BindFlow，一款基于Python的软件，用于在自由能微扰（FEP）或分子力学–泊松-玻尔兹曼/广义Born表面能[MM(PB/GB)SA]理论水平上自动执行绝对结合自由能（ABFE）计算。BindFlow是一款免费、开源、用户友好、易于定制的工具，可在工作站或分布式计算平台上运行，并提供详尽的文档和教程。BindFlow使用GROMACS作为分子动力学引擎，并内置支持小分子力场GAFF、OpenFF和Espaloma。我们通过计算139对配体/靶点的亲和力来测试一下BindFlow，这些配体/靶点涉及八个不同的靶点，包括六个可溶性蛋白质、一个膜蛋白和一个非蛋白质的主客体系统。通过皮尔逊、肯德尔和斯皮尔曼相关系数进行量化后，我们发现BindFlow的预测与实验结果的一致性总体上与该领域的金标准相当。有趣的是，我们发现MM(PB/GB)SA在某些体系和力场下所能达到的相关性，已接近自由能微扰法（FEP）的结果，而所需的计算成本仅为后者的一小部分。本研究确立了BindFlow作为经过验证且易于使用的ABFE计算工具。
