# Prolonging Sample Stability with Machine Learning Expands the Catalog of Tests Available for Mail-In Capillary Blood Collected At-Home

**通过机器学习延长样品稳定性扩展了可用于在家中收集的邮寄毛细血管血液的测试目录**

## 精华总结
一种基于软件的方法通过机器学习模型预测和校正样品降解，延长了居家采集血液样品的稳定性，扩展了可用的检测项目范围，支持去中心化临床试验和公共卫生筛查。

**关键词**: 机器学习、样品稳定性、居家血液检测、质量控制校准

## 摘要（英文）
Self-collection of biospecimens at-home, without specialized equipment or training, are increasingly being adopted in clinical practice due to convenience and patient preferences. However, sample instability during shipment means that remote access to common blood tests remains challenging. We hypothesized that the inaccuracy and imprecision in test results that develop because of sample instability could be modeled and controlled using knowledge of transit conditions captured by environmental sensors. We subjected 2685 blood samples from 65 participants to temperature cycles derived from real-world transit conditions. Training a model called Remote Control to predict change enabled accurate calibration of test results to approximate the time zero value at the point of collection, despite sample degradation. With calibration, unprocessed whole blood could be transported, for up to 9 days under ambient conditions and exposed to temperatures between 3.4 and 47.4 °C. Under these conditions, agreement with CLIA TEa ranged between 98.1 and 100%, with a |%bias| of 0.1-1.6%, a %CV of 2.2-4.9%, and a minimum sigma metric between 3 and 8.8σ for lipids (Cholesterol, HDL, LDL, Triglycerides, APO-A1, and APO-B). Performance was linear across measurement intervals (R2, 0.929-0.995), accurate at medical decision levels (MCC, 0.808-0.981), with uniform model error under differing laboratories, equipment systems, reagent chemistries, patient demographics, health status, and medication status. Importantly, performance generalized to an independent cohort of participants collecting capillary blood. This software-based approach to prolong sample stability expands the catalog of tests available at-home, supports decentralized clinical trials and public health screening initiatives, and improves how healthcare can be affordably delivered at-home and on the patient's terms.

## 摘要（中文）
由于其便捷性及患者偏好，无需专业设备或培训的居家自采生物样本正越来越多地应用于临床实践。然而，样本在运输过程中容易发生不稳定，这使得远程获取常规血液检测结果仍面临挑战。我们假设，由于样本不稳定而产生的测试结果不准确和不精确，可以通过利用环境传感器捕获的运输条件知识来建模和控制。我们对65名参与者的2685份血样进行了模拟真实运输条件下温度变化的循环处理。训练一个名为“遥控”的模型来预测变化，使得尽管样本会发生降解，仍能对测试结果进行准确校准，以近似采集时的零时间值。经过校准后，未处理的全血可在环境条件下运输长达9天，并能承受3.4至47.4℃的温度范围。在这些条件下，与CLIA TEa的一致性范围为98.1%至100%，|百分比偏倚|为0.1%至1.6%，百分比变异系数为2.2%至4.9%，脂质指标（胆固醇、HDL、LDL、甘油三酯、APO-A1和APO-B）的最低Sigma度量介于3至8.8σ之间。各测量区间内的性能呈线性关系（R²=0.929~0.995），在临床决策水平上具有较高的准确性（MCC=0.808~0.981），且在不同实验室、检测系统、试剂化学体系、患者人口学特征、健康状况及用药情况等条件下，模型误差保持一致。重要的是，该性能在采集毛细血管血的独立受试者队列中同样适用。这种基于软件的方法可以延长样本的稳定性，扩大居家可用的检测项目范围，支持分散式临床试验和公共卫生筛查计划，并改善以可负担的方式在家中、按照患者意愿提供医疗服务的方式。
